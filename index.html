Research Themes

Five Research Directions

Spanning building physics, indoor environmental quality, and artificial intelligence — grounded in field measurement and validated on real buildings.

In-situ Building Envelope Diagnostics

Non-destructive field methods for measuring the real thermal performance of building envelopes — bridging the gap between design specs and as-built reality.

KEY TOPICS
In-situ U-value (ASTR method) Airtightness measurement Infrared thermography Heat-flux virtual sensors

Zero-Energy & Green Remodeling

Diagnosis-to-retrofit frameworks that turn aged buildings into zero-energy and green-certified assets through cost-optimized renovation packages.

KEY TOPICS
Zero-Energy Building (ZEB) Existing-building retrofit Green remodeling G-SEED / energy rating

Building Energy Modeling & AI Control

Physics- and data-driven modeling coupled with machine learning for predictive control, demand flexibility, and grid-interactive efficient buildings.

KEY TOPICS
BIM-BEM integration Model predictive control LSTM / deep learning BEMS & demand flexibility

HVAC, IAQ & Healthy Buildings

Ventilation, air-cleaning, and germicidal technologies that improve indoor air quality and occupant comfort while minimizing energy use.

KEY TOPICS
Indoor air quality (IAQ) UVGI & biosensors Electrostatic PM filters Natural ventilation & comfort

Energy Welfare & Carbon-Neutral Policy

Field programs and scenario analysis that improve energy efficiency for low-income households and inform national pathways to carbon neutrality.

KEY TOPICS
Energy welfare retrofit Weatherization (WAP) Carbon-neutral scenarios Building-sector GHG policy

Standards, Certification & Commissioning

Translating research into national standards, certification schemes, and ZEB system commissioning protocols adopted across the building sector.

KEY TOPICS
ISO TC163 contribution ZEB commissioning KOLAS testing Energy rating certification

Dr. Jonghun Kim is a Principal Research Scientist and Head of the Building Energy Group at the Korea Institute of Energy Research (KIER), and a Professor of Energy Engineering at the University of Science & Technology (UST). He earned his Ph.D. in Architectural Environment (energy & indoor air quality) from the University of Tokyo in 2011.

His research bridges building physics, indoor environmental quality, and artificial intelligence — developing in-situ diagnostic methods, building energy models, and intelligent control systems that turn existing and new buildings into healthy, zero-carbon environments. He holds international credentials including Certified Passive House Designer and Certified Measurement & Verification Professional.
